Welcome to FETP Bangladesh – Intermediate

The Intermediate Field Epidemiology Training Program (FETP) strengthens public health surveillance, outbreak investigation, and emergency response capacity in Bangladesh.

What is FETP (Intermediate)?

The Field Epidemiology Training Program (FETP) is a competency-based, service-oriented training program designed to strengthen national public health systems by developing skilled field epidemiologists. It follows the “learning by doing” model and is technically supported globally by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). FETP usually has three tiers:

  • Frontline (Basic) – District-level surveillance officers
  • Intermediate – Mid-level epidemiologists
  • Advanced – National-level epidemiologists and leaders
    • The Intermediate level focuses on:
  • Applied epidemiology
  • Biostatistics
  • Outbreak investigation
  • Surveillance system evaluation
  • Public health emergency response
  • Scientific communication
    • It is typically a 9–12 month fellowship, combining classroom modules and extensive field placement (≈70–80% fieldwork).

    FETP Intermediate in Bangladesh

    In Bangladesh, the FETP is implemented by the Institute of Epidemiology, Disease Control and Research (IEDCR) under the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare.

    Structure in Bangladesh (Intermediate Level)

  • Duration: ~1 year
  • Host institution: IEDCR, Dhaka
  • Technical collaboration: US CDC and regional partners
  • Field placement: District and national surveillance units

    • Core Competencies

  • Disease surveillance system management
  • Outbreak detection and investigation
  • Emergency public health response
  • Risk communication
  • Program monitoring & evaluation
  • Scientific report writing and presentation

    • Field Activities Include:

  • Leading outbreak investigations (e.g., dengue, COVID-19, cholera)
  • Evaluating national surveillance systems
  • Analyzing DHIS-2 data
  • Developing public health bulletins
  • Supporting emergency response coordination

    • Global Recognition of FETP Intermediate

    FETP is implemented in over 80 countries. It contributes to:

  • International Health Regulations (IHR 2005) core capacity development
    • Global Health Security Agenda (GHSA) workforce targets Strengthening epidemic intelligence

    Graduates are

  • Certified Field Epidemiologists
  • Engaged in WHO, CDC, UNICEF, ministries of health
  • Involved in global outbreak response teams

    • Importance of FETP Intermediate for Bangladesh

    Bangladesh faces:

  • High population density
  • Climate-sensitive diseases
  • Emerging zoonotic threats
  • Rapid urbanization

    • FETP Intermediate strengthens:

  • District-level surveillance capacity
  • Early outbreak detection
  • Evidence-based public health decision making
  • Health system resilience

    • It creates a skilled epidemiology workforce, which is critical for:

  • Pandemic preparedness
  • Antimicrobial resistance surveillance
  • NCD surveillance expansion
  • Digital health integration (e.g., DHIS-2)
